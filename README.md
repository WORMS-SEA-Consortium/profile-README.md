# WORMS-SEA: Watch for Resistance from Mass Drug Administration of Soil-Transmitted Helminthiases in Southeast Asia
Overview
The WORMS-SEA consortium investigates how mass drug administration (MDA) programs in Southeast Asia influence the evolution of anthelmintic resistance in soil-transmitted helminths (STHs). Our work spans Malaysia, Indonesia, and the Philippines, using a natural experiment created by differing national MDA policies. We combine field parasitology, drug efficacy testing, and whole-genome sequencing to understand how repeated anthelmintic use affects drug efficacy, how MDA shapes STH population genetic structure, and which genomic regions are under drug-driven selection. Our outputs support policymakers, public health programs, and the scientific community by generating actionable evidence on anthelmintic efficacy and resistance.
Objectives
• Measure anthelmintic efficacy in MDA vs. non-MDA regions
• Characterize STH population structure using whole-genome data
• Identify genomic signatures of selection associated with drug pressure
• Build a sustainable Southeast Asia–UK research network
• Strengthen regional capacity in genomic epidemiology and bioinformatics

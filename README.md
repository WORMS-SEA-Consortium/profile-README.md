# WORMS‑SEA: Watch for Resistance from Mass Drug Administration of Soil‑Transmitted Helminthiases in Southeast Asia
Overview
The WORMS‑SEA consortium investigates how mass drug administration (MDA) programs in Southeast Asia influence the evolution of anthelmintic resistance in soil‑transmitted helminths (STHs). Our work spans Malaysia, Indonesia, and the Philippines, leveraging a “natural experiment” created by differing national MDA policies.
We combine field parasitology, drug efficacy testing, and whole‑genome sequencing to understand:
• How repeated anthelmintic use affects drug efficacy
• How MDA shapes STH population genetic structure
• Which genomic regions are under drug‑driven selection
Our outputs support policymakers, public health programs, and the scientific community by generating actionable evidence on anthelmintic efficacy and resistance.
---
Objectives
• Measure anthelmintic efficacy in MDA vs. non‑MDA regions
• Characterize STH population structure using whole‑genome data
• Identify genomic signatures of selection associated with drug pressure
• Build a sustainable Southeast Asia–UK research network
• Strengthen regional capacity in genomic epidemiology and bioinformatics
---
Consortium Countries
• Malaysia — minimal or no MDA
• Indonesia — active MDA
• Philippines — active MDA
